## Strongest Behaviors
- **Ownership:** Takes end-to-end responsibility for outcomes from concept through iterations; reports directly to leadership and ships accountability
- **Strategic communication:** Able to frame disagreements in terms of stakeholder goals rather than insisting on being right (Unnati AI pivot example: reframed PWA overhaul around leadership's stated vision of digitizing undigitized India)
- **Learning from failure:** Consciously ships imperfect versions to learn, then pivots decisively when data contradicts assumptions
- **Intellectual curiosity:** Self-directed deep dives into systems (Bluetooth research for interview answer; statecraft and military strategy as personal interest)

## How You Work Best
- **Ambiguity and ownership:** Thrives when given direction (what to solve) but not prescriptive method (how to solve it); builds strategy from first principles
- **User-driven decision-making:** Energized by primary research (1:1s, melas, prototype testing) that validates or challenges assumptions
- **High agency in cross-functional work:** Leads without formal authority; comfortable coordinating backend, frontend, design, analytics, ops across multiple teams and multiple levels
- **Fast shipping culture:** Prefers rapid iteration and learning over endless planning; values shipping v1 to force real user feedback

## Growth Areas (frame positively in applications)
- **Hands-on technical depth:** Strong enough in Python/ReactJS to read code and spec correctly, but not a production engineer. In interviews: "I'm fluent enough to solve problems with engineers, but I'm strongest at strategy and product direction rather than becoming a specialist in any one tool."
- **Stakeholder influence at scale:** Excels with small teams (6-person DigiHaat launch, 80-team ONDC coordination). Uncertain how leadership and influence dynamics shift in much larger orgs (1000+ people).
